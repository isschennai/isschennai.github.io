About Program




By casually looking at the data on Education and Health for Tamilnadu, we can find that the state has got a good record in many areas on these two sectors and it is competing with Kerala for the top position. For example in school education, it accomplished 100% in the percentage of schools having book banks/reading room/ library, percentage of entitled children with special need receiving aids and appliances, percentage of school with headmaster/principal, percentage of secondary schools who have teachers for all core subjects, percentage of single teacher schools and percentage of schools having functional girls toilet. It also accomplished more than 90% in net enrollment Ratio (NER), the percentage of identified out-of-school-children mainstreamed in the last academic year. We can see a similar record in the health sector as well. For example, it is in the first position with regards to Neonatal Mortality Rate (NMR), Under-five Mortality rate (U5MR), Total Fertility Rate, Sex Ratio at Birth (SEB), Proportion of Institutional deliveries, etc., Why it is so? Is it something related to its historic strong social reform movement? It is something related to the quest for equality and social justice? The objective of this seminar is to analyze the socio-economic historic reason behind the significant advancement in these two sectors.

About Organisers




Indian School of Social Sciences, Chennai (ISSS) is a voluntary organization that facilitate a platform for discussion on intellectual topics. Though ISSS was formed in 1983, it has been registered as a society in 2018. ISSS organized various special Lectures and Seminars on Developmental Sciences, Social Sciences, Economic Sciences, and Political Science. ISSS has instituted memorial Lecture in the name of Legendary Trade Union Leader V.P. Chinthan and W.R.Varatharajan and other social activists. Moving forward, with the growing need of Intellectual thirst among the mass due to rapid increase in Literacy rate, ISSS would like to leverage the advancement of Technology to establish a website on its own. It has a plan to involve in research activities on developmental issues. It will intensify its effort to reach out to students, youth and workers.

Speakers

Mr. J Krishnamoorthy

Mr. J Krishnamoorthy is an eminent personality in Puducherry and Tamil Nadu with more than 36 years of experience in school education, starting his career in 1976 in Puducherry as a middle school teacher. Mr. Krishnamoorthy is a founding member of the Puducherry Science Forum (est. 1985) and was the State General Secretary of the Tamil Nadu Science Forum (1994 - 96). Mr. Krishnamoorthy also served as the manager of one the five Bharath Jan Vigyan Jathas which were cross-country marches held between 2nd Oct. – 7th Nov. 1987 to raise science awareness across the country. The marches were very popular and inspired the establishment of the National Literacy Mission and the Total Literacy Campaign. The Jathas also inspired the Puducherry Science Forum to start a children’s magazine, “Thulir” (“Flower Bud”) catering to children in Puducherry and Tamil Nadu.

Mr. Krishnamoorthy was a leading member of the Puducherry Total Literacy Campaign. Starting Sept. 1989, in a span of two years, he was instrumental in taking the Campaign to 1,00,000 people in 500 villages, training 10,000 volunteers, 1000 master trainers, and 100 key resource persons. The success of these efforts led Puducherry to be conferred the King Sejong International Literacy Award in 1992. Between 1992 - 96 he also served as one of the 35 national consultants for the Bharat Gyan Vigyan Samithi, to implement total literacy programs across the country. Mr. Krishnamoorthy moved back to Puducherry in 1996, to spend time with his family. From this point on, until his retirement in 2013, he solely focused on children's education as a teacher, and middle and higher secondary Govt. school headmaster.

Mr. Krishnamoorthy is the founding member of Pallikalvi Paadhukaappu Iyyakkam ("Save School Education Movement"; est. 2017) and serves as its Secretary for Tamil Nadu and Puducherry. The Movement's goal is to increase the quality of education in Govt. schools by leveraging school management committees to ensure proper oversight and accountability. Currently, the Movement partners with about 40 organizations, and is led by about 15 prominent educationists, and 15 eminent headmasters, teachers, and education volunteers.


Dr.S.Kasi .M.S.M.Ch

Dr.S.Kasi .M.S.M.Ch an eminent and experienced Paediatric Surgeon, trained in the most up to date minimally invasive Advanced Paediatric Procedure and Paediatric Endo Urology procedures.

He received his medical degree from Madurai Medical College, Madurai Kamaraj University. Did his post graduation from Tirunelveli Medical College. He specialized in Paediatric Surgery from Institute of Child Health, Madras Medical College. He had been the faculty member of Children’s Hospital, Egmore since 2001 for more than 13 years.

He is one of the pioneers in Chennai to practice the Single Stage Laparoscopy Assisted Transanal Pullthrough procedure as against the conventional three stage procedure for Hirschsprung’s disease (congenital megacolon).

He is one of the leading doctors who fought for the revival of closed public sector vaccine producing units at Guindy, Coonor And Kasauli in 2009-10.

He has presented several free papers and has been invited to give several scientific presentation at local, national and international conferences.

An able and dynamic administrator, he is efficiently managing the only Paediatric surgical hospital in the suburban Chennai (Tambaram) - Dr. KK Surgical and Paediatric centre at East Tambaram, Chennai. with his team of dedicated Paediatric surgeons, Pediatric Anesthesiologists, Paediatric Gastroenterologist, Paediatric Neurologist and Paediatric Intensivists, under one roof and is delivering outstanding results with exceptional care.
